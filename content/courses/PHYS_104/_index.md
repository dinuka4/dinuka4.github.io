## Course Description

This course follows the well-worn route of the first semester of introductory physics, a course that teaches the basic elements of forces and motion. This involves using mathematical models to solve classic problems involving projectiles acted upon by gravity, objects that collide and spin, and connecting heat to motion. 

## Main Course Objectives

* What are the basic physical quantities that can be measured? How do accuracy and precision  differ?
* What is velocity? What is acceleration? How can they characterize motion along a straight line?
* What is a vector? How can vectors allow us to describe the motion of projectiles in three dimensions?
* What is a force? How do Newton’s laws connect force and motion, mass and acceleration?
* What is energy? What are the types of energy? How does energy relate to position and motion?
* What is momentum? What is angular momentum? How are the two related to each other?
* What are conservation of energy and conservation of momentum? How do these constrain motion?
* What is Newton’s Law of Universal Gravitation? How does this relate planets’ motion to forces?
* What is heat? What is temperature? How do these relate to the motion of particles in a gas?
